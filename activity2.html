Activity 2 — Biomass-based Biogas System (100 Cattle)

A complete farm-scale biogas plant that converts cattle dung into methane-rich biogas to supply electricity and heat for on-farm needs; digestate is used as organic fertilizer.

Conversion process

Collected dung → mixing tank (slurry) → anaerobic digester (mesophilic/thermophilic) → biogas formation (CH₄ + CO₂) → gas storage → biogas engine/CHP → electricity + heat. Digestate separated/used as fertilizer.

Key components

  • Manure collection & slurry mixing tank
  • Plug-flow or mixed anaerobic digester (sized by retention time)
  • Gas holder (fixed dome / flexible bag)
  • Biogas engine or micro-CHP unit
  • Digestate storage and fertilizer processing

Sizing estimate (100 cattle)

Average dung per cow ~12–15 kg/day. For 100 cows, expected biogas yield ~6–8 m³ per cow per day → total ~600–800 m³/day. A 50–100 kW CHP unit would convert this for farm loads (exact sizing depends on retention time and feedstock mix).

Applications & benefits

  • On-site power for lighting, milk cooling, water pumping
  • Heat recovery for cleaning and water pre-heating
  • Reduces methane emissions and manages waste
  • Produces high-quality organic fertilizer (digestate)
