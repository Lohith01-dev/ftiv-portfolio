Activity 3 — OTEC / Geothermal Hybrid (Electricity & Freshwater)

A conceptual design that uses ocean thermal gradients or hot water wells to produce continuous baseload power. The system can be hybridized with desalination for freshwater production.

Conversion process (OTEC / closed-cycle)

Warm surface seawater heats a low-boiling working fluid (ammonia) in the evaporator → vapor drives a turbine-generator → vapor condenses using cold deep seawater → working fluid recirculated. For geothermal, hot water/steam drives turbines directly or via binary cycles.

Key components

  • Warm-water intake and evaporator
  • Working fluid loop and turbine-generator
  • Cold-water intake (deep seawater) and condenser
  • Desalination module (optional) and heat exchangers
  • Intake/outfall structures and anti-corrosion materials

Sizing & feasibility notes

OTEC requires ~20°C temperature difference between surface and deep water for practical efficiency; a small pilot plant (50–200 kW) is suitable for island communities. Geothermal sizing depends on reservoir temperature and flow rates — binary cycles suit moderate temperatures (90–150°C).

Applications & impact

  • Base-load electricity for islands/coastal villages
  • Co-production of desalinated water for communities
  • Low-carbon continuous generation compared to intermittent renewables
