Activity 1 — Tidal Power Plant (Coastal Village)

This design uses tidal barrage/stream turbines to provide predictable renewable power to a small coastal community (lighting, clinic, communications, pumps).

Conversion process

Sea tidal motion → water flow through sluice/gates → turbine rotation → generator → power conditioning (transformer/inverter) → distribution to loads. Optionally combined with short-term battery storage to smooth supply.

Key components

  • Site-specific barrage or tidal channel
  • Low-head turbines (Kaplan / bulb / axial flow)
  • Powerhouse with synchronous/asynchronous generator
  • Control gates, transformer, switchgear
  • Optional battery bank or small microgrid inverter

Sizing estimate (example)

For a small village (~50 households + clinic): A turbine array of 100–250 kW peak could be appropriate depending on local tidal range. Preliminary hydrodynamic and site surveys are required for final sizing.

Real-world relevance

Tidal power is highly predictable (astronomical cycles) making it ideal for coastal critical services. Environmental mitigation (fish-friendly turbines, sediment management) must be considered during design.
